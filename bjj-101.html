You’ve never trained Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu before – or maybe it’s been a while – and you feel like you need to start from the top, the very top; That’s exactly where BJJ 101 gets going. BJJ 101 is the gateway to learning your way around the mats, learning a little self-defense in the process and building a real appreciation for a discipline that has captivated the attention of casual practitioners and professional mixed martial artists alike.

SO WHO SHOULD TAKE BJJ 101?

  • Anyone new or returning to the sport. BJJ 101 is a crash course (or a refresher) for the curious and the brave.
  • Anyone interested in learning the best way to practically defend yourself. Familiarize yourself with the discipline which has revolutionized martial arts in America and across the world.
  • Anyone looking to lose weight and become more fit. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu yields profound improvements in cardiovascular conditioning, physical endurance, strength and muscle tone in a remarkably short period of time.

Coursework in this introduction classes covers philosophical and conceptual distinctions, terminology, etiquette, dress and the fundamental positions of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. More specifically, you’ll learn to tie your belt, how to train safely with your partner, a little bit of history about the art and some of the very basics of grappling. Perhaps even more importantly, you’ll meet your instructors and coaches as well as your training partners. Learn, their names – It’s not the last class you’ll share together!

EXAMPLE SYLLABUS (DAY ONE):

On day one all new students should arrive at least 15 minutes prior to class beginning. If you already own an appropriately fitting Gi (a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu training kimono not unlike a Judo uniform) feel free to bring it with you. Otherwise, come dressed in a comfortable t-shirt and athletic shorts. Upon arrival a new student will be shown the appropriate dressing room and will be instructed as to which mat the first class will take place on.

SUBSEQUENT COURSE MATERIAL

Each BJJ 101 course session is unique and builds upon the previous coursework already taught. You’ll want to make sure you start at the first class in the series. If however you are unable to attend a class, BJJ 101 is repeatable as many times as you would like – There’s no limit. Many students find that the taking the course more that once is a valuable experience and helps fortify important fundamental concepts and positions in their Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu journey.

Students should expect to complete the BJJ 101 course with a healthy grasp of a variety of topics including elementary self-defense, positional escapes, and positional transitions as well as a handful of low-impact throws/takedowns.

USE BJJ 101 AS A STEPPING STONE TO OUR MAIN PROGRAM.

At the conclusion of the 8 week class, students are invited to attend the all-level classes. This is a great opportunity to meet more senior students and see how they train, conduct themselves in class and to observe more advanced techniques.

Additionally, graduates are offered a special rate on a single private lesson.
